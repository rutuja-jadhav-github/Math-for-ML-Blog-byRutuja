"Time is like a flower, Krishna said once. I didn't understand. But later I visualized a lotus opening, the way the outer petals fall away to reveal inner ones. An inner petal would never know the older, outer ones, even though it was shaped by them, and only the viewer who plucked the flower would see how each petal was connected to the others."

— Panchaali, The Palace of Illusions by Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni

I was brought up surrounded by art— dancing, painting, writing, fashion, film, music— you name it. Art wasn't just a hobby, it was how I made sense of the world.

When I studied Engineering, I could solve the problems (often solve them well, in fact). But I couldn't see through them. Math lived in textbooks; it felt detached from the messy, creative world that interested me. So I gravitated towards a career where I could use the lens I already had—people, products, experiences, and ideas.

Years later, while working with AI systems and studying data science, I encountered mathematics differently. Slowly I was able to see the creativity and intuition behind equations: how simple ideas combine, transform, and reappear to explain remarkably sophisticated concepts.

I often find myself circling back to this quote from one of my favourite books, because it captures something I've observed about learning. While we're in the middle of it, ideas often feel isolated. Only with time do the connections become visible. Looking back, I can trace a line between art and engineering, creativity and mathematics, uncertainty and lived experiences. What once felt like separate petals now feels like a single flower unfolding.

Mathematics for Machine Learning is like a lotus. Every idea carries the ones before it— compressed, invisible, yet enriched by its forebearers. You arrive at algebra without remembering how probability and calculus shaped your intuition, or apply gradient descent without noticing the geometry beneath it.

This blog endeavours to trace those connections, one petal at a time.

Fair disclaimer: I'm a novice here. This blog is a learning journal—an attempt to understand and explain the concepts I'm studying at University, hopefully in a way that helps others in the same boat, and helps me too.
